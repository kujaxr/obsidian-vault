Scope 
This TL standard specifies requirements for factory-fill engine oil with corrosion 
inhibitors. 
2 
Requirements 
2.1 
Basic specifications 
Engine oils according to this TL standard offer optimum performance as factory-
fill oil for initial break-in operation of spark ignition and diesel engines, including 
engines with exhaust gas turbo-charging, and for regular service, provided that 
the oil change intervals specified in the owner's manual are followed. 
The release is valid for a period of 3 years. 
When the release is revoked the supplier is obliged to forward this information to 
the concerned parts suppliers of the VW Group. 
Approval of first supply and changes acc. to VW 011 55 "Vehicle Supply Parts - 
General". 
Avoidance of hazardous substances acc. to VW 911 01 "Vehicle Environmental 
Standard".

Notes on testing and explanations 
1) If the phosphorus content is ≥ 0.08 to 0.10 % P, functional and/or in-vehicle tests may be 
required for release. Type and scope of these tests shall be coordinated with the VW Central 
Laboratories. 
The specified P content of the oils refers to type ZnDDPanti-wear additives. 
2) Only necessary for sample testing. 
3) Source of supply for the test elastomer: 
FKM "SRE AK 6" 
Parker-Prädifa GmbH 
4) For the test runs use a fuel batch that has yielded a mean value of ≤ 8.5 points in the respective 
CEC team PL-053 inter-laboratory test using reference oil RL 140. Only results from test stands 
which have successfully taken part in the given valid inter-laboratory test of the CEC-PL 053 and 
for which the deviation in inter-laboratory testing with the RL 140 reference oil is not greater than 
the simple standard deviation from the mean value (x ± 1 s) shall be accepted for VW factory-fill 
engine oil releases. The CEC-PL 053 shall be informed of the validity of the inter-laboratory test for 
the VW factory-fill engine oil releases by the Central Laboratory (ZL). 
The test duration can be deduced from the currently valid CEC team PL 053 inter-laboratory test, 
depending on the severity of the fuel batch used. 
5) The requirement for the number of points to be attained with the oil under test (nK) on the 
respective test stand is deduced from the test stand value obtained with RL 140 (nPST RL 140) and 
the standard deviation (s) from the inter-laboratory test: 
nK = nPST RL 140 + 2 s. Since this would mean that unrealistically high requirements for the oil under 
test would be obtained mathematically on individual, approved test stands, the minimum 
requirement to be attained is ≥ 9.0 points. 
The same fuel batch approved by the CEC team PL 053 and the VW Central Laboratory (ZL) shall be 
used as the test fuel for the oil under test and reference oil. Any test conditions that may deviate 
from the specified test procedure are subject to prior approval by the VW Central Laboratory (ZL). 
6) The same fuel batch approved by the VW Central Laboratory (ZL) shall be used as the test fuel for 
the oil under test and reference oil. Sources of supply and fuel batch shall be stated in the test 
certificate.
