Scope 
This standard specifies requirements for the following types of multicoat paints for decorative 
corrosion protection of cast and forged alloy wheels: 
TL 239, Type B 
for aluminum-look alloy wheels (e.g. wheels turned to give a polished 
finish, barrel-polished wheels) with clear coat, colored areas with 3-
coat paint finish 
TL 239, Type C 
 
for alloy wheels with 3-coat paint finish 
TL 239, Type D 
for alloy wheels with 4-coat paint finish for color W90 (royal silver) 
2 
Description 
Description example for surface protection of an alloy wheel that is turned to give a polished finish 
and painted: 
Surface protection according to TL 239-B

3.2 
Appearance 
On the front surface that is visible in as-installed condition, the parts shall have a uniform 
appearance in conformity with the drawing and the released sample. The visual appearance of the 
wheel surface must not be impaired by paint flaws, casting defects, mechanical flaws or rework. 
Pores, cracks, a mottled appearance, orange-peel effect or other flaws are not permissible. 
Maximum deviation samples will be defined in two-day production prior to introduction into standard 
production. The requirements of Test Specification PV 6607 apply to wheels turned to give a 
polished finish.

3.4 
Coating thicknesses 
See Table 2. The test results of all specifications must be documented in the first-sample test 
report. 
The specified coating thickness values apply to the surface. Coating thickness values that fall 
below the minimum coating thickness due to the shape of the parts are permissible if the 
requirements in Table 3 are met. 
The total coating thickness is determined using the eddy current test method according to DIN EN 
ISO 2360. 
The individual coating thickness values are determined by means of microsection examinations 
using a reflected light microscope according to DIN EN ISO 1463. 
Alternatively, the thickness of the paint coats can be determined using the “paint borer” (e.g. type 
518) from Erichsen.

Bitumen and tar remover 
Mix 5 g Panmex bitumen “85/25” made by the ESSO company with 5 ml petroleum ether to yield a paste. Subsequently cover an 
area of the part under test with a diameter of approx. 30 mm with this paste. After an exposure period of 1 h, the covered area is 
cleaned of the tar paste with the tar cleaner (no. 000.096.052 C). 
2) 
Sealant and sealant remover 
One section is coated with sealant wax by immersion. After 24 h aging at room temperature, the wax coating is rinsed for 3 min 
in petroleum heated to +80 °C by keeping the part i n motion during immersion. The standard production sealant application 
procedure and standard production sealant removal procedure can also be applied for testing. 
3) 
The test shall be performed with wheel weights 1K0.601.178.C or 1K0.601.178 D (supplier: Dionys Hofmann/Albstadt). 
The rim must be cleaned with special boiling point spirit according to DIN 51631-2 before the weights are adhered. Dry cleaning 
is to be performed with a clean fabric rag (cotton). The weights are to be positioned so that – during the pull-off test – the 
maximum force is reached before the weights touch the all-around bead of the adhesive surface limit. The weights shall be 
pressed on for 5 s with a force of 15 N (e.g. using a spring force measuring device). 
For the pull-off test, the wheel is mounted with the visible side facing down. After a waiting time of 4 h or 24 h (standard climate), 
the pull-off test shall be performed with a speed of v = 10 mm/min. 
NOTE: The limit values for shear stresses have been selected so that variations in the adhesive tape quality have been taken 
into consideration. This is intended to avoid a separate test of the adhesive weights.
