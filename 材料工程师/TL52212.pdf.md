1 
Scope 
This Technical Supply Specification stipulates the material requirements for rubber parts that only 
come into contact with brake fluid when a leak occurs, e.g. protective caps for brake master 
cylinders, drum brakes (wheel brake cylinders) and floating calipers. 
2 
Designation 
Protective cap according to TL 522 12

6 
Notes on testing 
The glass vessels required for the tests can be acquired from the Society of Automotive 
Engineers, Inc., 400 Commonwealth Drive, Warrendale, PA 15096, USA. 
6.1 
Aging at elevated temperature 
S3A standard specimens according to DIN 53504 as well as 3 finished parts (for Shore A hardness 
and evaluation) are aged for 166 h in the mechanical circulation oven according to DIN 53508. 
Measurements are performed 30 min after removal. 
Test temperatures: 
Protective caps for brake master cylinders: 
 
+100 °C 
Protective caps for drum brakes and floating calipers: 
+140 °C 
6.2 
Aging in brake fluid 
S3A standard specimens according to DIN 53504 as well as 3 finished parts or finished part 
sections (for Shore A hardness and evaluation) up to 5.7 ml in total volume are aged for 70 h in 
75 ml of the standard production brake fluid released by Volkswagen. 
For this purpose, tightly closable glass vessels with approx. 250 ml volume and 50 mm diameter 
according to ISO 4927 shall be used. After the test is over, the rubber parts are conditioned in the 
vessel at (23 ± 5) °C for 60 to 90 min. Subsequently, the specimen s shall be removed from the 
liquid, rinsed briefly (max. 10 s) in isopropyl alcohol or ethyl alcohol and dried with a clean, fiber-
free cloth. The measurements shall be performed within 60 min after the specimens have been 
rinsed in alcohol. 
Test temperatures: 
Protective caps for brake master cylinders: 
 
+100 °C 
Protective caps for drum brakes and floating calipers: 
+140 °C

6.3 
Aging in lubricant 
S3A standard specimens according to DIN 53504 are aged for 70 h in lubricant according to 
TL 521 50. 
The total volume of the standard specimens aged in a vessel to the volume of the test fluid shall be 
in the ratio 1 : (80 ± 5) (see PV 3323); measurements are performed 30 min after removal from the 
lubricant. 
Test temperatures: 
Protective caps for brake master cylinders: 
 
+100 °C 
Protective caps for drum brakes and floating calipers: 
+140 °C 
6.4 
Tensile strength and elongation at tear 
Testing is performed with S3A standard specimens according to DIN 53504. 
The tensile strength of the aged standard specimen shall be based on its cross section before 
aging; the elongation at tear shall be based on its condition after aging. 
6.5 
Low-temperature resistance 
Aging for 144 h at -40 °C to -43 °C. Load applicati ons and measurements take place in the cold 
chamber. 
6.6 
Ozone resistance 
Ozone concentration:  
(50 ± 5) pphm 
Test temperature: 
 
(40 ± 2) °C 
Test duration:  
 
(70 ± 2) h 
The specimen parts shall be mounted the same way as in normal operation and aged at room 
temperature in this state for (22 ± 1) h. The tests in the ozone device shall then be performed. 
6.7 
Change in weight after aging at elevated temperature 
The finished part is suspended and aged for 94 h in the natural convection oven according 
to DIN 53508. It is weighed 30 min after removal. 
Test temperatures: 
Protective caps for brake master cylinders: 
 
+100 °C 
Protective caps for drum brakes and floating calipers: 
+140 °C 
6.8 
Extractable ingredients 
For each determination, one finished part or finished part section with a weight of up to 1.0 g shall 
be extracted for 8 h with n-hexane, then left for 16 h at room temperature in the system (extractor 
without solvent) and then extracted again for a further 8 h. After 30 to 60 min airing at room 
temperature, the specimens shall be dried for 48 h at +80°C in the natural convection oven 
according to DIN 53508.
