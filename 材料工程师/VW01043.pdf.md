Preface 
The screw industry produces two types of 12-point drive shapes that are not interchangeable: The 
multipoint socket profile, based on three offset square profiles and the internal 12-point profile, 
based on two offset hexagonal profiles. 
 
The multipoint socket profile according to this standard is a drive shape for threaded parts that can 
be assembled and disassembled with a tool repositioning angle of 30° based on the internal 
engagement geometry.

Scope 
This standard is to be used for pan head bolts similar to DIN EN ISO 4762, DIN 6912, or DIN 7984, 
DIN EN ISO 7045, oval head bolts according to VW 604 11, flat head bolts according to 
VW 604 04, and flange bolts according to VW 604 28. This standard shall also be used for non-
standardized threaded parts requiring a minimum wall thickness of “y min” and "Y' min.", see 
Figure 2, Figure 3, Figure 4, Figure 5, and Table 1. If pan head bolts and flange bolts with 
multipoint sockets are used, their drive profile generally must be one nominal size larger in order to 
be able to transfer the higher tightening torque for assembly beyond the elastic limit. 
Example: M10 – N12
