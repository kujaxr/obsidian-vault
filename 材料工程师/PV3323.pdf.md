1 
Scope 
This test specification defines the test vessels and test racks to be used to age standard 
specimens according to DIN 53504 in liquid media, and the ratios by volume of the specimen to the 
test fluid to be maintained. 
2 
Description 
Specimen holder according to PV 3323 
Test vessel according to PV 3323 
3 
Specifications 
The total volume of the standard specimens aged in a vessel to the volume of the test fluid shall be 
in the ratio 1 : (80 ± 5). 
The standard specimens suspended from the specimen holder must be surrounded by the test fluid 
such that there is at least 10 mm between the standard specimen and the bottom of the test vessel 
or the surface of the test liquid. The test fluid must surround each standard specimen to a lateral 
depth of at least 5 mm. 
It must be possible to cover the test vessel against external air such that no major changes in 
pressure occur in the vessel when the specimens are heated up and cooled off and that the air in 
the test vessel is not exchanged during aging at constant temperature. 
4 
Test equipment 
4.1 
Vessels 
Tall glass beakers according to DIN 12331, nominal contents 100, 150, 250, 400, 600 and 
1,000 ml, deviating from this standard, however, with no spout and with flat ground edges. 
Beakers whose nominal contents deviate from DIN 12331 are also used. 
The beakers are covered with glass plates. 
The beakers to be used are specified in Table 1. 
The English translation is believed to be accurate.

4.2 
Racks 
Fixtures with a circular base and a bar made from rustproof metal (see material specifications 
Figure 1) shall be used. Pointed bars for holding the specimens are attached radially to the bar at 
three levels (Figure 1). The bottom level of the specimen holder is intended for S3 standard 
specimens, the middle level is intended for S3a standard specimens and the top level is intended 
for S2 standard specimens. As well as this “combination” rack design, racks with only one 
specimen holder level can also be used.
