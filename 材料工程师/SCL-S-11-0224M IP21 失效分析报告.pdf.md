Ⅱ. 结论与建议 Conclusions and suggestions 
结论Conclusions： 
 加强板是由于多源的疲劳断裂失效的；主要起源于外（底）表面，距离隔套约2mm。次要疲劳裂纹开始于隔套
前面的边部。The panels have failed due to multi-origin fatigue fracture, which was primarily initiated on the outer (bottom) surface of the 
panel, approximately 2mm rear of the sleeve.  Secondary fatigue cracks were then initiated at the forward edge of the sleeve. 
 加强板表面，尤其是左侧，显示加强板和副车架的安装脚之间有明显的相对运动痕迹。这表明连接处的夹紧载
荷不充分。The panel surfaces, particularly the left hand side, show clear signs of relative movement between the panel and the subframe 
mounting foot.  This indicates insufficient clamping load in the joint. 
 隔套的顶部表面没有接触上面板的迹象。这种情况必须通过检测上面的面板来证实。如果没有接触，连接是不
安全的；它将表现为低的拆卸力矩。The top surfaces of the sleeves show no sign of contact with the upper panel.  This must be 
checked by inspection of the upper panel. If no contact, then the joint was not secure, This would also manifest as a low disassembly torque. 
 差的焊接工艺导致隔套和加强板之间出现间隙以及小的过载裂纹。但是过载裂纹本身对疲劳开裂没有影响。Small 
overload cracks were found near to the welds, these have resulted from poor welding process, leading to a gap between sleeve and panel.  
The overload cracks themselves did not contribute to the fractures. 
 材料方面没有表现出对疲劳不利的因素。There is no material-related factor for the fatigue. 
建议Suggestions： 
 检查隔套和上面板之间间隙的情况。Check gap condition between sleeve and upper panel 
 改进焊接工艺，以消除加强板与隔套之间的间隙。Improve welding process to eliminate gaps between panel & sleeve 
 确认测试件具有足够的夹紧力。Confirm clamping load is sufficient for test conditions.

Cut 1 
源区1 在一个电阻焊接的附近。横截面如图4 所示，几点值得注意：  
 
疲劳裂纹在垂直加强板的方向进行传播。断裂后断口表面已经严重受损，所以不可能确定裂纹的扩展方向。金相
组织是正常的。 
 
裂纹通过焊接材料和热影响区。该裂纹的形态和方向显示其原因是过载而不是疲劳。  
 
可能有两个原因：1.隔套和加强板的间隙显示：在焊接凝固中，压力没有保持，收缩应力导致裂纹的产生。 2.在
紧固的过程中，间隙变小，焊缝处出现较大的应力，从而导致裂纹的出现。 
 
上汽不接受有裂纹的零件；但这些裂纹并没有导致疲劳失效。 
 
加强板的厚度为1.85 毫米。考虑容许的原料厚度公差（2 毫米±0.14）以及变形中减薄，该厚度是可以接受的。 
 
显微硬度测试结果显示：焊接过程不影响加强板表面硬度。 
Origin area 1 is near to one of the resistance welds.  The cross-section is shown in figure 4 and several features are important to note: 
 
The fatigue crack has propagated directly perpendicular to the panel.  The fracture surfaces have been heavily damaged after fracture, so 
it’s not possible to identify fracture direction. The microstructure is normal. 
 
There are cracks through the weld material and into the heat affected zone. The morphology and direction of the cracks shows that these 
are overload fractures (not fatigue). 
 
There are two possible causes: 1. The gap between the sleeve and panel shows that pressure was not maintained during weld solidification, 
and so the resulting contraction stress led to the cracks. 2. The gap was compressed during torque-tightening, and the resultant stress led 
to the cracks. 
 
Although the cracks are not acceptable in SAIC components, they did not lead to the fatigue failure.  
 
The panel thickness of 1.85mm is acceptable, considering the tolerance of the raw material (2mm±0.14), and slight reduction due to the 
forming process.  
 
Microhardness testing showed that the panel surface hardness is not affected by the welding process.
