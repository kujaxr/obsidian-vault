control tests of these characteristics.

GENERAL 
The finishing for plastic material components dealt with in this Procurement Specification can be obtai-
ned by a traditional painting process (spraying of one or more painting products), by deposition, e.g. 
tampography, by hot stamping, or realized during the molding operations, e.g. dye-painting (utilized for 
the decoration of the steering wheels) or making use of in-molding and insert-molding technologies. 
Therefore, the ”aesthetic finishing” can be carried out on any vehicle inside and outside finishing parts 
and its classification depends on the resistance of materials and technology utilized for its execution. In 
order to achieve the aesthetic and functional targets and to ensure the surface look of the part after 
molding (presence of embossing, photoengraving of the mold, molding defects) sometimes the surface 
has to be treated in advance making use of agents that facilitate the adhesion and filling or aesthetic 
primers before applying the true aesthetic finishing.  
Therefore, the characterization of the utilized materials, technological process and required aesthetic 
features is carried out on the finished part, according to the procedures described in this document. 
 
The painted products utilized for the vehicle inside finishing must be FREE of diacetone alcohol 
and mesethyl oxide (even as impurity in the base products). 
If the specific solvent for the painting product is acetone, its utilization is allowed provided that 
it is FREE of the above-said solvents.

2.4 
Visual inspection 
The look of the part that is being tested shall be continuous and even, free of surface and aesthetic de-
fects. The aesthetic contents (look, color, brightness) shall be the ones defined by the indefinite stan-
dard or by the shared and deposited reference sampling (as per Procedure 08094).  
2.5 
Thickness 
2.5.1 
The thickness measurement is carried out by micro-graphic method. The measurement is carried out in 
section and, therefore, a sample of at least  1 sq. cm. shall be taken from the part that is being tested. 
The measurement can be carried out applying the special equipment (PIG) directly to the part, after 
cutting the layers until uncovering the support. 
2.5.2 
Compare the measured values with the limits possibly prescribed in the relevant ”Specifications Data 
Sheet”, indicated on drawing or in the specific document issued by the Supplier and attached to the 
Self-qualification Test Plan (Procedure 07740).

Brightness 
2.8.1 
Measure the brightness of the part as it is according to Standard 50457. If the measurement cannot be 
carried out directly on the part, obtain a specific sample applying the aesthetic finishing on a support 
whose characteristics permit to carry out the measurement. 
2.8.2 
Compare the average of 3 measures with the limit prescribed in the relevant ”Specifications Data 
Sheet”, indicated on drawing or in the specific document issued by the Supplier and attached to the 
Self-qualification Test Plan. 
2.9 
Accelerated aging under atmospheric agents and subsequent adhesion 
2.9.1 
Get 1 sample of dimensions 150  70 mm from the part to be tested and submit it to aging for 1500 
hours in W.O. with Xenon lamps (as an alternative, for 1000 h in W.O. with carbon-arc lamps) accor-
ding to the methodology described in Standard 50451. 
2.9.2 
At the end of aging, check the conditions of the sample visually after reconditioning it at environmental 
temperature for: 
- 2 hours with "SOLVENT TECHNOLOGY" coating cycles 
- 6 hours with "WATER TECHNOLOGY" coating cycles”  
 At the End of the test compare measured values with the prescribed limit. 
2.9.3 
Measure adhesion according to Standard 50461 and brightness according to Standard 50457 on the 
sample after polishing the surface to be measured slightly with polishing paste, compare the results 
with the limits prescribed in the relevant ”Specifications Data Sheet”. 
NOTE: As concerns brightness as new  70 gloss carry out the measurement without polishing the 
surface to be measured.
