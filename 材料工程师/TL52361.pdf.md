1 
Scope 
This Technical Supply Specification defines requirements for elastomer compounds used for hoses 
in the coolant circuit. The hoses are suitable for the temperatures occurring in the coolant system (-
40 °C to +150 °C; up to +160 °C for brief periods).  In addition, the hoses are available with an 
inside layer stabilized against electrochemical corrosion.

3.2 
Appearance 
The hose inside surface must be free of defects and must not exhibit any unevenness. Flaws, such 
as blisters or inclusions, in the total structure are not permissible. 
The hoses shall be free of stains, bleached areas, crystalline deposits and gray cast. 
The individual layers shall exhibit proper bonding even after they have been exposed to the fluids. 
The hoses shall be free of parting agents and soiling, such as elastomer particles and fibers.

7 
Notes on testing 
7.1 
Specimen preparation 
For specimen preparation, the coolant hose is cut into inside and outside layer using a bandknife 
splitting machine. 
7.2 
Aging at elevated temperature 
S3A standard specimens acc. to VDA 675 205 which have been removed from the outside layer of 
the coolant hose are aged in a mechanical circulation oven acc. to DIN 53508. 
Measurements are performed 30 minutes after removal. 
7.3 
Coolant aging 
S3A standard specimens acc. to VDA 675 205, which have been taken from the inside layer of the 
coolant hose, are aged in an autoclave for 94 hrs at +160 °C in test fluid which consists of 50 
volume percent de-ionized water and 50 volume percent coolant additive acc. to TL 774-D. 
The fluid volume shall be 150 times to 180 times the specimen volume. The specimens are cooled 
down to room temperature in the test fluid and subsequently measured.

7.4 
Aging in diesel fuel 
The specimens are aged for 46 hrs at (23 ± 2) °C in standard diesel fuel (e.g. Haltermann Co., 
A20/NP II) with 5 volume percent rapeseed oil methyl ester added, optionally in diesel fuel acc. to 
TL 788-B. The liquid volume shall be (80 ± 5) times the specimen volume. Test containers and 
specimen holders according to PV 3323 shall be used. 
After completion of the aging period, the specimens shall be removed and any fluid shall be wiped 
off immediately by means of filter paper. The weight change in the specimens shall be measured 1 
minute later, followed by the tensile strength, elongation at tear, and hardness test. 
7.5 
Tear propagation strength 
The test shall be performed in deviation from DIN ISO 34-1, method A concerning the following 
aspects: 
─ 
Specimen thickness: 
≥ 1 mm 
In the case of coolant aging, proceed acc. to Section 7.3. The volumetric ratio of fluid to specimen 
can be ignored. 
7.6 
Ozone resistance 
Hose sections (as hose ring) of 20 mm width are compressed with a cock with screwed shank until 
the inner surfaces touch, then they are aged in this condition. The specimens shall be brought into 
an ozone climate chamber 15 min after mounting the clamp. 
7.7 
Permanent deformation 
The specimens shall be prepared by cutting with a reinforced circular cutter acc. to DIN 53516 from 
the finished part ends, going from the hose inside layer to the hose outside layer. The hose outside 
layer shall face downwards in the specimen holder. 
Unevenness in the hose outer layer shall be ground plane in the area which is to be measured. 
7.8 
Delamination resistance 
The test shall be performed in deviation from DIN 53530 concerning the following aspects: 
─ 
Specimen width:  
20 mm 
─ 
Specimen removal: 
in longitudinal direction of the hose 
─ 
Evaluation: 
 
Specification of xmin., xmax., arithmetic mean value, standard deviation 
The hose section shall be clamped into the specimen holder with the outside layer facing 
downwards. 
In the case of coolant aging, proceed acc. to Section 7.3. The volumetric ratio of fluid to specimen 
can be ignored.
