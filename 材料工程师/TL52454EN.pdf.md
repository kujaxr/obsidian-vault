Scope
This standard defines the requirements for belt webbing for safety belts.
Description
Description example for a calendered belt webbing for 3-point seat belts:
PET according to TL 52454-D
Requirements
General requirements
Approval of first supply and changes according to Volkswagen standard VW 01155.
Emission behavior acc. to VW 50180, and VW 50179 (Audi).
Avoidance of hazardous substances according to VW 91101.
For a complete examination, at least 40 m of belt webbing or belt ASSYs containing the same overall
belt length are required.
The ends of the belt webbing must be processed in such a way as to protect them against fraying.

Notes on testing
Tear strength
According to U.S. Regulation 571.209, Section S 5.1 (b) and ECE Regulation No. 16, Section 7.4.2.
Elongation
According to U.S. Regulation 571.209, Section S 5.1 (c).
Abrasion
According to U.S. Regulation 571.209, Section S 5.1 (d).
Carbon-arc
According to U.S. Regulation 571.209, Section S 5.1 (e) and ASTM G 23-93.
High-temperature light exposure
According to ECE regulation No. 16, Section 7.4.1.2, maximum tensile force after xenon arc light
aging according to DIN EN ISO 105-B02. The belt webbing must be exposed to the light until the blue
scale level has changed to an extent that corresponds to the contrast of gray-scale level 4 acc. to
DIN EN 20105-A02. This effect is achieved after 2 high-temperature light exposure cycles according
to PV 1303.
Aging at elevated temperature
The seat belt webbing must be attached in the forced ventilation oven in a free-hanging manner using
yarn made from the same material as the belt webbing.

Filter paper test
A specimen of approximately 25 cm2 is wetted with 2 cm3 of deionized water, placed between filter
papers (Schleicher & Schüll, "Blauband 589") and 2 glass plates, and loaded with a weight of 5 kg
for 1 h. The filter paper bleeding is evaluated according to the DIN EN 20105-A03 gray-scale level.
