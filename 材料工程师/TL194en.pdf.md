1 
Scope 
This Technical Supply Specification (TL standard) specifies requirements for electrolytically 
deposited zinc coatings with additional Cr(VI)-free organic/inorganic non-metallic sealings on 
ferrous materials according to code letter c of Volkswagen standard VW 137 50, which are 
predominantly in contact with magnesium and therefore require surface protection that is tailored to 
the effects of magnesium. 
Coatings as specified in Technical Supply Specification TL 194 shall be used where heavy 
corrosion protection is required (protection class 6). They are not however permitted for joining 
elements ≥ Fk 10,9 or > 1000 MPa.

3.2 
General requirements 
Approval of first supply and changes according to VW 011 55. 
Avoidance of hazardous substances according to VW 911 01. 
10 finished parts are required for complete testing. 
Unless certain sections of a part that are marked in the drawing are excluded from the surface 
coating, the entire surface of the parts must comply with the required surface protection type and 
display the prescribed properties. The coatings shall adhere firmly to the base material. 
Prior to coating, threaded parts shall exhibit tolerances corresponding to the coating thickness – 
metric internally threaded parts according to VW 116 24, metric external threads according to 
VW 116 27. After the coating process, the h-tolerance must not be exceeded for external threads 
and the H-tolerance must not be fallen below for internal threads. 
The production process and its control shall not impair the functional characteristics of the finished 
part. 
The protective coatings must not exhibit any pores, cracks, damage, or other flaws that impair the 
corrosion protection or specified appearance. 
Given proper mounting, the coating shall not be damaged in any way that would lead to impairment 
of function or of the specified corrosion protection. 
The surface treatment procedure shall be conducted so that damage by delayed hydrogen-induced 
brittle fractures can be ruled out. Verification by the bracing test according to DIN 50969 in the first-
sample test report. 
3.3 
Joining elements 
For screws, nuts and quick fastening elements, the test requirements with respect to base metal 
corrosion for the surface finishes listed below apply only to the head and wrench bearing surfaces; 
in the case of similar threaded and shaped parts, such as e.g. tap end studs, only for the face 
surfaces. 
The reduced requirements according to Section 5.2.2 apply to process-related weak spots of the 
coating on joining elements such as the shank and the thread. 
Furthermore, the specifications in DIN EN ISO 4042 concerning the maximum possible thickness of 
the electroplated coating in the threaded profile shall be taken into consideration. 
These reduced requirements also apply to quick fastening elements. 
The coefficients of friction are tested according to VW 011 29. 
For joining elements with metric thread, the seal with integrated sliding agent shall be used for 
standard applications. 
For further information see VW 011 10.

Structure, coating thickness 
4.1 
Structure 
4.1.1 
Substrate material 
According to drawing 
4.1.2 
Electrolyte 
Zinc coating shall be made from slightly acidic or alkaline electrolytes; the zinc layer must not be 
contaminated by incorporated iron. 
4.1.3 
Conversion layer 
Thick layer passivation is used to improve the corrosion resistance. 
The passivation treatment solution must not contain any Cr(VI) compounds in order to ensure that 
the resultant conversion layers are likewise Cr(VI)-free. 
4.1.4 
Sealing 
The seal shall be applied after passivation, either wet-on-wet or within 4 hours. 
The thickness of the layer structure can be increased slightly by the additional sealing, but this 
must not impair the functionality. 
Especially in those areas visible for the customer the outer appearance of the parts must not be 
impaired by strong color variations or iridescence. 
The post-treatments must not cause any impairments such as unsightly drop-like residues resulting 
from crystallization or formation of a film on the part surface. 
If besides the corrosion protection further functional surface characteristics (such as paintability, 
compatibility with agents, sliding properties, vulcanizability) are required, part-specific tests or 
functional tests shall be performed.

5 
Properties 
5.1 
Adhesive strength 
Thermal shock test based on DIN EN ISO 2819. 
The specimen part is aged for 30 minutes at 220 ± 10 °C and then dipped in water at a temperature 
of 15 to 25 °C. 
Requirement: no large-scale or bubble-shaped stripping of the zinc coating. 
5.2 
Corrosion behavior 
Testing according to DIN 50021-SS; evaluation according to DIN EN 12329. 
The corrosion resistance of the systems must be ensured in the as-received condition and also 
after a 24-hour period of aging at an elevated temperature of 120 °C. These are minimum 
requirements and shall always be adhered to.
