II. 结论与建议 Conclusions and Suggestions 
 
The parts have failed due to pre-existing cracks.  The cracks were created during processing, either during forming, 
or between pressing and sintering, due to accidental damage.  Ten new parts from batch 1 did not show any 
abnormal defect after mechanical testing.  The supplier must check their process and explain how the damage 
occurred. 
 
The Acoustic NDT test has not been fully calibrated for this type of part.  The supplier must make trials with 
purpose-damaged parts to ensure that the results are meaningful.  Mechanical testing may not be a useful test 
to detect defects. 
 
The material composition & density were according to the drawing, but variations in density were found within the 
parts.  The supplier must aim for a uniform density throughout the part.

these joining points fracture, leaving a typical ductile (or brittle) fracture surface (figure 3).

surfaces (figure 4).

Supplier testing of new parts (NDT & mechanical test) 
 
The supplier has the possibility to check their parts using an acoustic test, and has recently been checking all new 
parts (Batch 1 was not tested).  In order to validate this acoustic test and check for defective parts, mechanical 
testing was carried out. 
 
Three types of samples were tested: 
1. 34 Parts (from different batches).  Acoustic test = Not OK.   
2. 20 Parts from batch 1, which were kept by the supplier as initial samples.  Acoustic test = OK. (only 10 parts 
were then mechanically tested) 
3. 30 Parts produced on 2011.04.01. Acoustic test = OK. 
 
All parts were weighed before testing, as a comparative assessment related to density.  Mechanical testing to 
failure was then carried out, as shown in figure 6a. 
   
 
 
        
         
 
Figure 6 Schematic of mechanical test.   
a) Test jig   b) Some typical crack locations after testing  c) Cracks seen in vehicle failures 
 
 
After testing, the maximum load to failure was recorded, and the fracture features were assessed. Most parts 
cracked around the edge of the platen, and/or directly through the boss (figure 6b).    
 
On initial examination, none of the parts displayed fracture characteristics or defects the same as the vehicle 
failures (figure 6c).  However, on using the SEM, some parts showed areas with very little or no ductile fracture.

The results of this testing show:  
1. There is a positive correlation between weight (density) and strength 
2. The batch one parts have a lower weight (density), but this has been improved in subsequent batches 
3. For most parts which failed the Acoustic test, no clear reason was found.  The samples are in the middle of 
the weight range and did not fail at a low load.  Therefore the mechanical test might not be appropriate. 
4. Some samples did show unusual fracture surfaces, even though they failed at a high load (see figures 8-10)
