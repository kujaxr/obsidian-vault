Ⅱ. 结论与建议 Conclusions and suggestions 
结论Conclusions： 
1. 
弹簧为疲劳断裂，起源于表面上制造商标。 The springs failed through fatigue, initiated at manufacturing marks on the surface. 
2. 
沿着弹簧钢丝发现有其它的疲劳裂纹，表明首要原因是载荷过大。这可能是由于门闩系统磨损或腐蚀了，增
加拉手上的载荷。Additional fatigue cracks were found along the wire, indicating the primary cause is excessive load.  This may be 
due to wear or corrosion in the door latch system, increasing the load on the handle. 
3. 
轻微的表面腐蚀是疲劳裂纹的萌生点。 Light surface corrosion created initiation points for the fatigue cracks. 
4. 
弹簧材料没有规范，但金相组织和硬度是典型的。No specification was found for the spring material, however the material and 
hardness are typical. 
5. 
拉手#C 弹簧无裂纹。No cracks were found in the spring from handle #C 
 
建议Suggestions： 
1. 
调查开门装置载荷过大的原因。Investigate source of increased load in door opening mechanism. 
2. 
在弹簧制造中，必须避免表面缺陷。 Surface defects must be avoided in spring manufacture. 
3. 
提高弹簧的耐腐蚀性（用蜡或油脂）。 Improve the corrosion resistance of the spring (use wax or grease)

III. 调查结果 Investigation Results  
 
三件门拉手被提供用于分析。样品A 和B 都失效了。样品C 也提供，但没有发现故障。 
失效的两个产品表现出类似的特征，都因疲劳而断裂；裂纹起源于有制造缺陷的表面（图1-3）。疲劳扩展的深度
约为直径的1/3，剩余截面因过载而断裂失效。 
Three door handles were submitted for analysis.  Samples A & B had failed.  Sample C was also submitted aslthough no fault was found.  
The two failed parts showed similar characteristics. Both had failed through fatigue, initiating from a manufacturing defect at the surface (figures 
1-3). The fatigue crack propagated approximately 1/3 of the wire diameter before the remaining cross section failed in overload.

显微分析Microstructural analysis 
拉手＃A 弹簧断裂处的金相照片显示：疲劳扩展区是非常平整，具有典型的疲劳特征，起源于一个小的腐蚀坑（图
3）。腐蚀后，在同一平面上，可以发现一些较小的疲劳裂纹；它们起源于非常小的表面腐蚀坑，深度约〜10μm
（图4）。 
裂纹的数量表明：疲劳失效的主要的原因不是表面缺陷，而是弹簧负载过高。 
A cross-section through the spring from handle #A showed a typical flat fatigue crack,m originating at a small corrosion pit (figure 3).  On etching, 
a number of smaller fatigue cracks could be found in the same plane, also originating from very small surface corrosion pits, ~10µm deep (figure 
4). The number of cracks indicates that surface defects are not the primary cause of the fatigue, but that the load on the spring is too high.
