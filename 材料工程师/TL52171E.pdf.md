fluids for life filling in manual transmissions under high load.

3.2 
Type 
Transmission oils for optimum transmission performance in manual transmissions during winter 
and summer operation. 
Synthetic oil-based technology with additives to improve viscosity/temperature behavior and 
synchronous compatibility as well as the ability of the oil to protect against wear, oxidation, foaming 
and corrosion. 
3.3 
Miscibility 
Transmission oils according to this specification shall be miscible with one another in any ratio 
without limitation. The mixtures themselves shall also comply with all requirements of this 
specification. 
Conformity evidence is based on a mixing ratio of 50 : 50.

Release procedure 
For a laboratory release within the framework of technical engineering release testing, the lubricant 
manufacturer shall provide 10 l of lubricant to the Operating fluids laboratory along with a VDA 
(German Association of the Automotive Industry) first-sample test report and the related test 
certificates for the requirements specified in Sections 6 to 9. Other tests, such as function tests and 
road trials are required for technical engineering release. These require 5 000 l of material. 
Only a tested and released additive batch may be used for blending in the first sample. For the 
first-sample test, the supplier of the transmission oil shall provide 10 l of the end product (mixed 
according to standard-production conditions) to the Quality Assurance department of the plant 
placing the order, and 1 l to the Operating Fluids Laboratory; samples are to be accompanied by a 
VDA first-sample test report. 
The evaluation statement automatically expires 3 years after release has been granted or when the 
formulation of the transmission oils is changed. 
The release shall remain in effect given regular deliveries and verification of quality control 
according to test schedule. The Quality Assurance department of the ordering plant shall receive a 
plant testing certificate (DIN EN 10204) with each delivery. The plant testing certificate 
encompasses the manufacturing and fill report numbers as well as the test values according to the 
test schedule in Table 2. A test report according to Table 2 shall also be submitted to the Operating 
Fluids Laboratory every year.

The supplier is obliged to ensure that each delivery batch meets the requirements specified in this 
Technical Supply Specification. Each additive batch shall be tested prior to use for blending in the 
end product. If the supplier of the transmission lubricant removes one or more separate partial 
quantities from the total quantity of a manufacturing batch at different times and delivers them to 
the ordering party or stores them, each partial quantity shall be designated as its own fill batch and 
given a fill report number in addition to the manufacturing report number. 
When delivering in drums, only clean drums shall be used. For bulk deliveries, the tanker shall be 
cleaned suitably before filling. 
Changes in the formulation, the manufacturing method, the upstream supplier or the manufacturing 
location shall be communicated to the Operating Fluids Laboratory, to the laboratories of the 
affected plants, and to the relevant engineering department responsible for transmission 
development; they necessitate new sampling and a new written release. If the release is revoked,
