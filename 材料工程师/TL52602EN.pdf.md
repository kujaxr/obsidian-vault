3.2 
Appearance 
The hydrolysis-resistant PUR foamed plastic must foam uniformly and largely free of voids. 
Deviations from the shrinkage cavitation requirements must be approved by the lead engineering 
department and the responsible laboratory. 
On principle no processing problems must occur on the assembly line as a result of the respective 
foam structure and existing shrinkage cavitation (e.g. through tearing or tear propagation of the 
foam surface). 
Adhesive equipment may be required according to the drawing. 
Amine emissions of the foamed plastic system used must correspond to the indicator method acc. 
to Test Specification PV 3937. 
The molded parts must maintain their dimensional and functional integrity during transportation, 
storage, assembly, and subsequent service in the temperature range from -30 ºC to +90 ºC as well 
as under climatic conditions of +55 ºC and 95 % relative humidity. 
3.3 
Physiological compatibility 
The material components must be physiologically safe and must not induce skin irritation or 
objectionable odors, for example. 
Substances which are emitted at elevated temperatures shall be toxicologically safe.

5 
Notes on testing 
The specimens required to determine the mechanical/technological characteristic data are taken 
from the molded parts (components). 
The minimum foam thickness is 10 mm. 
The required component-specific specimen-removal points and measurement ranges for non-
destructive testing shall be stipulated and presented in the corresponding component drawing. 
5.1 
Gross density 
Testing according to DIN EN ISO 845. 
The preferred specimen dimensions are (70 x 70 x 30) mm. 
5.2 
Compression stress 
Compression stress test acc. to DIN EN ISO 3386-1 at 40 % deformation (thickness reduction) 
taking into consideration PV 3919. 
Evaluations are performed for 
! 
components in the area of application without direct crush stress: 
1st and 4th compression 
! 
components in the area of application of direct crush stress: 
1st, 4th and 
10th compression 
Aged parts are measured directly after removal. 
5.3 
Moisture aging 
The following is defined as a cycle under this test procedure: 
5 h aging at +120 °C and 100 % relative humidity or saturated steam with subsequent drying for 
3 h at + 70 °C. 
5.4 
Compression test 
Non-destructive component testing acc. to PV 3919, Section 4.4.3. 
For measurement ranges and requirements see component drawing. 
5.5 
Thickness gaging 
The difference between the measured initial thickness and the thickness before the 4th and 10th 
compression is used as the dimension for the thickness reduction after loading. 
The test is performed based on PV 3919. 
Requirements apply to specimen and component testing.

5.6 
Tensile strength 
The test is performed in consideration of DIN EN ISO 1798, but with a specimen based on tensile 
specimen A (e.g., with Zwick punching tool No. 8722). 
! 
Total length: 
103 mm 
! 
Specimen thickness: 
10 mm 
! 
Web width: 
10 mm 
! 
Measuring length: 
30 mm 
The use of dimensionally modified specimens due to the geometric conditions on the component is 
permissible in coordination with the responsible test laboratory. 
The test is performed with a traverse speed of 100 mm/min. 
5.7 
Adhesive strength 
The adhesive strength is determined in the form of shear force determination. 
Specimens with the dimensions (50 x 50 x 20) mm are placed between two dip-primed test panels 
of (50 x 100) mm. 
The specimen with the non-adhesive surface is affixed to a test panel using double-sided adhesive 
tape. 
The specimen is pressed on for 1 min with 10 N in the area where the sheets overlap (25 cm2) 
Next, the protruding test panel ends are clamped in a tensile test machine. 
The shear force is determined with a test speed of 25 mm/min. 
The test panels used must be free of grease and cleaned, e.g. with ethanol.
