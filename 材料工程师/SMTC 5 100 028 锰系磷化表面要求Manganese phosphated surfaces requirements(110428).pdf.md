3 
Requirements 
If the following requirements for specific 
components or parts of components do not ap-
ply completely or not at all, this shall be coordi-
nated with the responsible engineering depart-
ments and a special note shall be added to the 
drawing. 
3.1 一般要求 
根据SMTC 90008，避免有害物质。 
 
3.1  General requirements  
Avoidance of hazardous substances acc. to 
SMTC 90008. 
3.2 结构 
含晶质红磷锰矿的锰系磷化层必须均匀、完
整地覆盖金属表面。不允许盐脱水（形成白色斑
点）。 
 
3.2  Structure 
The manganese phosphate layer, com-
prising crystalline hureaulite, must cover the 
metal surface evenly and entirely. Dehydration 
of salts (formation of white flecks) is not permit-
ted. 
3.3 色彩 
深灰至黑色。允许小量色差。 
 
3.3  Color 
Dark gray to black. Minor color variations 
are permitted. 
3.4 镀层厚度 
测试根据 DIN EN ISO 2178 使用磁选法或使
用光学显微镜。要求：2 至8 μm。 
 
3.4  Layer thickness 
Test using the magnetic method according 
to DIN EN ISO 2178 or using the light-optical 
microscope. Requirement: 2 to 8 μm. 
3.5 结晶规格 
使用扫描电子显微镜在600:1 的放大截图下
查看。通过将特性值分配到图1~图6 中的其中之
一进行确定。要求：长度：（2 ~12）μm，厚度（1~6） 
μm，特性值：1~3。 
 
3.5
Dimensions of the crystals
The examination is conducted using the 
scanning electron microscope at a magnification 
of 600:1. The characteristic value is specified by 
allocating it to one of the pictures, 1 to 6. Re-
quirements: Length: 2 to 12 μm, thickness: 1 to 
6 μm, characteristic value: 1 to 3.

3.6 酸洗痕 
取各部件的 4 个齿面在未侵蚀的横向薄切片
（90°至齿面）上放大 200x 和 500x 进行研究。
最大酸洗痕深度不得超过 30 μm。要求： 
允许有个别酸洗痕（参见图 7 和图 8）。不允许
酸洗痕之间太靠近、深度大于 10 μm、一起形成
波纹表面光泽或者每毫米的数量超过 5 个（参见
图 9 和图 10）。 
 
 
3.6 Pickling marks
The study is conducted on an unetched 
transverse microsection (90° to the tooth flanks) 
at 200x and 500x enlargement on 4 tooth flanks 
(thrust and traction flanks) for each component. 
The maximum pickling mark depth must not 
exceed 30 μm. Requirements: Individual pick-
ling marks are permitted (see figures 7 and 8). 
Pickling marks that are close to one another 
have a depth of > 10 μm and together produce a 
wavy surface finish and whose number exceeds 
5 per mm, are not permitted (see figures 9 and 
10).
