Notes on testing 
6.1 
Wet boiling point 
The basic method is defined in FMVSS 116. 
The following devices are required for water absorption: 
─ 
Desiccators, inner diameter 250 mm, with lid for sampling 
─ 
Desiccator plates, Section 6.3 – Figure 1, diameter 235 mm 
─ 
4 SAE RM-49 corrosion test containers 
─ 
1-ml measuring pipette with plunger 
─ 
Natural convection oven 
The desiccator is preconditioned, i.e. is filled with (450 ± 25) ml of distilled water and placed in the 
oven without specimens at (50 ± 1) °C 1 day prior t o beginning testing. The unglazed porcelain 
plate will thus become saturated with water. 
350 ml of the brake fluid to be tested and, respectively, of TEGME test fluid are weighed into one 
corrosion test container each. The TEGME test fluid shall be adjusted to 0,5% water content at the 
start of testing. Two corrosion test containers, one containing the brake fluid to be tested and one 
containing TEGME, are then placed upon the desiccator plate of the preconditioned desiccator next 
to one another. The central hole of the plate shall remain unobstructed. 
Before the desiccator is closed, a magnetic stirrer is added to each of the specimens. The desicca-
tor is then placed back into the oven at (50 ± 1) °C. 
Beginning after 10 h of aging, 1 ml of the TEGME specimen shall be removed every 2 h and its 
water content determined. Before removal, the TEGME shall be homogenized by stirring with the 
pipette. The pipette tips shall be placed in a dry desiccator. The water contents determined in this 
manner shall be entered in a diagram in relation to time and used for the extrapolation of the pre-
sumed final point of 3,7% water. After the aging period resulting from this, the two fluids (the brake 
fluid to be tested and the TEGME) are removed from the desiccator and placed upon a magnetic 
agitator for homogenization with the magnetic stirrer. Subsequently, the water content of each fluid 
is determined. 
After this, the boiling point is read from the boiling profile curve and the determined water content. 
6.2 
Water content after aging at 81% relative humidity 
Water absorption is carried out according to Table 1 No. 16, however, at (24 ± 0,5) °C and with 
150 ml of test fluid consisting of saturated ammonium sulfate solution [(425 ± 25) g (NH4)2SO4 and 
125 ml of distilled water]. 
Exposure to sun radiation shall be avoided. 
After the test, the diagram of boiling point in relation to aging period shall be determined from the 
boiling point/water content and water absorption/aging period diagrams. 
The exact measurement points can be interpolated from the measurement values gathered.
