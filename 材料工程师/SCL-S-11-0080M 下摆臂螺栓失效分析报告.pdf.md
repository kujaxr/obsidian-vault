Ⅱ. 结论与建议 Conclusions and suggestions 
 
结论Conclusions： 
1、1#螺栓属过载断裂。由断口表面和螺牙已经被腐蚀，可知螺栓为早期断裂失效。由该辆车没有其他抱怨(车
运行过程中无故障记录)，可知螺栓是在装车过程中发生的过载断裂。螺栓硬度符合技术要求。 
1、Bolt #1 has failed through overload.  Since the fracture surface and thread area show significant corrosion, the failure occurred some time ago.  Since there 
was no other complaint about the vehicle (no incident during running). The bolt must have been over-tightened and failed during vehicle production.  The hardness 
met the drawing requirements. 
2、2#螺栓受异常载荷，致使螺栓过载； 夹紧力丧失, 最终导致螺栓变形。 
2、Bolt #2 was therefore carrying an abnormal load and was overloaded.  The clamping load was lost and the bolt deformed. 
 
建议Suggestions： 
请校核螺栓的装配、加紧过程。 
The tightening process for these bolts must be checked.

III. 测试结果 Test Results

Figure 1  PAM showing location of bolts.  Tightening torque ∆2 is given as 140-170Nm 
  
螺栓1#  Bolt #1 
 
仅有螺栓的螺纹段提交中心实验室 (见图2)。螺栓的断裂位置和方式是典型的过载断裂。断口腐蚀严重，但比较
光滑，塑性变形比较少， 以上表明螺栓的热处理无异常。硬度检查结果符合10.9 级的技术要求 (见表1)。依据
硬度检测结果，可知失效螺栓超载需要120KN 以上的载荷。 因为其他的车轮或减震装置没发生问题，所以车辆
正常行驶时不会产生超过夹紧力和螺栓的抗拉强度的载荷。 
螺纹上有白锈 (白色腐蚀锌)和红锈(红色铁腐蚀)， 表明螺栓已经断裂了很长时间，并且水能很容易的进入螺纹连
接处(与2#螺栓对比：如果夹紧力正确，螺纹不易被腐蚀)。 1#螺栓螺纹有红锈表明面涂层已经脱落， 表明（近
头的位置：与2#螺栓对比)螺栓夹紧力较大。 
 
综上所述，我们认为导致失效的过程是在装配过程.，因此请校核螺栓装配过程和装配设备。 
 
Only the threaded end was returned to QAL (figure 2).  The fracture position and direction is very typical of overload failure.  Although the surface is very corroded, it 
is relatively smooth and there is little plastic deformation, this indicates that the material was heat treated correctly.  A hardness test confirmed the bolt meets the 
requirement for Grade 10.9 (table 1).  According to the hardness values, the load required to break this bolt is over 120kN.  Since no other complaint about the 
wheel or suspension was made, it is unlikely that such high loads occurred during normal driving, to overcome the clamping force and bolt tensile strength.  
 
There is both white (zinc) and red (iron) corrosion on the whole threaded area.  This indicates that the bolt has been fractured for a long time and water could get 
inside the thread (compare with Bolt #2 – if the joint is under correct clamping load, then the thread cannot corrode).  The iron corrosion indicates that the protective 
coating on the thread has been removed, therefore the bolt had been tightened enough to remove the coating (compare bolt #2, thread near to the head).   
 
It is therefore believed that the bolt was broken during production assembly.  The tightening process and equipment must therefore be checked.
