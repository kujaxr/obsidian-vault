Ⅱ. 结论与建议 Conclusions and Suggestions 
结论Conclusions： 
1、钥匙内部的金属圆盘通过多源双向弯曲疲劳开裂，从而导致永久变形。The keys have failed through multi-origin 
reverse bending fatigue cracking of the internal metal discs, which led to permanent deformation. 
2、裂纹开始在高应力区域，没有发现明显的缺陷。The cracks were initiated at areas positions of high stress. No 
obvious defects were found. 
3、触点和壳体有显著地磨损。这种磨损可能会限制接触盘的运动，在操作过程中导致高于正常的应力产生。
Significant wear of the contacts and housing was seen, this wear might restrict the movement of the disc, leading to 
higher than normal stress during operation. 
3、未提供材料要求，但是很少使用的圆盘和失效圆盘无明显的硬度或金相组织差异。No material specifications 
were provided, however comparison between little-used and failed discs showed no differences in hardness or 
microstructure. 
4、发现硬度特别高，脆性现象可能影响抗疲劳性能。 The disc hardness is particularly high, embrittlement 
phenomenon may influence fatigue resistance. 
建议Suggestions： 
1、 供应商应使用CAE 进行完整的应力分析，改善设计以提高抗疲劳性能。计算时要考虑磨损的影响。 The 
supplier must make a full CAE stress analyis investigation, and improve the fatigue resistance of the design.  The 
effect of the wear must be considered during calculations.  
2、对比其它OEM 钥匙的设计Benchmark other OEM’s key design.

材料分析Material Analysis 
材料规格没有提供，所以不能给予一个完整的评估。圆盘结构如图11 所示。从金相组织和硬度分析来看，圆盘
材料为冷轧不锈钢。使用XRF，证实表面层为银。 
为了研究在使用中材料是否发生变化，对两个钥匙＃7 和钥匙＃11 的所有圆盘进行了金相组织和硬度检测。结果
如图12 和13 所示。平均硬度为610HV0.5，变动是在实验误差范围内。这表明盘之间的，或靠近裂缝的材料，
没有任何显着的硬度变化。 
一个接触点的硬度为〜210HV0.3（图14）。 盘和接触点有400HV 硬度差别，可以解释接触点显著磨损。 
No material specification was provided, so a complete evaluation could not be given.  The disc structure is shown in 
figure 11. From the microstructure and hardness analysis, the disc material is cold rolled stainless steel. The surface layer 
was confirmed as silver using XRF. 
In order to investigate the possibility of material changes during use, all discs from both Key #7 and Key #11 were 
checked for microstructure and hardness. The results can be seen in figures 12 and 13. The mean hardness is 610HV0.5, 
and variations are within experimental error. This shows that there was no significant hardness difference between the 
discs, or of the material near to the cracks.   
The hardness of one contact surface was measured at ~210HV0.3 (figure 14).  The 400HV difference in hardness 
between disc and contact can explain the significant wear of the contact.
