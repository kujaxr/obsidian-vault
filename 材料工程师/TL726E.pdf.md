1 
Scope 
The present standard contains the lubricant requirements for transmission fluids, primarily for 
manual transmissions and axle drives with spur gear teeth and bevel gears for life filling. 
2 
Description 
Description example for SAE 75W transmission oil with friction modifier: 
Transmission oil acc. to TL 726-Z 
3 
Requirements 
3.1 
General requirements 
Approval of first supply and changes according to Volkswagen standard VW 01155. 
Avoidance of hazardous substances according to VW 91101. 
3.2 
Type 
Technology based on mineral oil with additives to improve viscosity/temperature behavior and 
synchronous compatibility as well as the ability of the oil to protect against wear, oxidation, foam 
and corrosion. 
3.3 
Miscibility 
Transmission oils in compliance with this standard shall be miscible with one another in any ratio 
without limitation. The mixtures themselves shall also correspond to all requirements of this 
standard. 
Documentation shall be provided for a mixing ratio of 50: 50.

4 
Release procedure 
For a laboratory release within the framework of technical engineering release, the lubricant 
manufacturer shall provide 10 l to the Volkswagen Group Laboratory along with a VDA first-sample 
test report and the related test certificates for the requirements specified in Table 3 Nos. 6 to 9. 
Other tests, such as function tests and road trials are required for technical engineering release. 
These require 1 000 l of material. 
For mixing a first sample, only a checked and released additive batch may be used. For the 
first-sample test within the framework of technical engineering release, the supplier of the 
transmission oil shall provide 1 l of the end product mixed according to the standard production 
conditions along with the associated VDA first-sample test report. 
The evaluation statement automatically expires 3 years after release has been granted or when the 
formulation of the transmission oils is changed. 
The release shall remain in effect given regular deliveries and justification of quality control 
according to test schedule. The Quality Assurance department of the ordering plant shall receive a 
plant testing certificate (DIN EN 10204) with each delivery. The plant testing certificate 
encompasses the manufacturing and fill report numbers as well as the test values according to the 
test schedule in Table 2. A test report according to Table 2 is also to be submitted to the 
Volkswagen Group Laboratory every year.

Technical Supply Specification. Each additive batch shall be tested prior to use for mixing the end 
product. If the supplier of the transmission lubricant removes one or more separate partial 
quantities from the total quantity of a manufacturing batch at different times and delivers them to 
the ordering party or stores them, each partial quantity shall be designated as its own fill batch and 
given a fill report number in addition to the manufacturing report number.
