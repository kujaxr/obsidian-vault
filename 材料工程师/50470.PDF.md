1
SCOPE
Method used to assess the water resistance of paints applied to specimens of components (*) as di-
rected in applicable Procurement Specification.
(*)
For specimens obtained from part/components, mask cut edges with flexible vinyl plastic adhesive tape
(P.S. 9.55367 type F).
2
PRINCIPLE
Method consists of submerging a paint film in water for a given period and at a given temperature and
of evaluating change in mechanical properties and appearance.
3
TEST PROCEDURE
3.1
Using heptane (Std. 56605/18), degrease specimen or part/component painted with product under test.
3.2
Immerse specimen vertically using an inert material support, into the thermostatic tank (ref. § A---1) filled
with distilled water previously heated to 60 " 2 !C (unless otherwise specified on Standards or specific
Procurement Specifications).
3.3
After 24 hours (or multiples of 24 hours as indicated on applicable Procurement Specification) remove
the specimen, dry with blotting paper, inspect it visually.
3.4
Leave the specimen to stabilize at ambient temperature for 2 h for metallic specimen or 6 h for plastic
material.
3.5
Assess physical – mechanical properties (hardness, adhesion) as indicated on applicable Procurement
Specification and compare results with properties measured prior to immersion in water.
4
INTERPRETATION OF RESULTS
Physical properties and surface appearance after test shall be within the limits required on Procurement
Specification relating to product under test. Generally, unless otherwise specified, hardness and adhe-
sion shall not be #10% of pre---test values.
