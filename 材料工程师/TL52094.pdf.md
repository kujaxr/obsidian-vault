3.2 
Appearance
Surface structure and color acc. to original sample.
The material must be fray-proof, i.e., it must not fray or break out at the cut edges during process-
ing or in later use.
The latex backing must adhere uniformly to the needle-punched, nonwoven material, must not
loosen from the material without damage and must not crumble during driving operation.
The material must not adhere at room temperature or at elevated temperatures.
The material must be flexible in the temperature range from –40 to +90° C and adapt well to the
installation position.
3.3 
Physiological compatibility
The trim material must be physiologically safe and must not induce skin irritation, for example, or
objectionable odors.
3.4 
Color and type
Acc. to original sample.
3.5 
Identification
Acc. to VW 105 40–6.
3.6 
Conditioning
Prior to testing, the specimens must be conditioned for at least 24 h in the DIN 50 014 – 23/50–2
standard climate.

3.9 
Material and structure
3.9.1 
Type without index
Nonwoven material of 100% PP, spin-dyed, quality 1a; binding using needle-punching and back
impregnation; in addition, the nonwoven fabric is coated with a latex foam (waffle backing); the
back can, if required in the drawing, include an anti-slip coating acc. to the release.
3.9.2 
Type A
Nonwoven material of 100% PP; binding using needle-punching and back impregnation; a binder
that is stable in shape is used; the back can, if required in the drawing, include an anti-slip coating
acc. to the release; the nonwoven material can be bonded with a mixed-fiber hardened felt card-
board acc. to TL 524 13.
3.9.3 
Type B
Nonwoven material of 50% PP and 50% PET or alternatively 100% PET; alternatively 70%
PET/30% PP; binding using needle-punching and back impregnation; a binder that is stable in
shape is used; the back can, if required in the drawing, include an anti-slip coating acc. to the re-
lease; the nonwoven material can be bonded with a mixed-fiber hardened felt cardboard acc. to
TL 524 13.
3.9.4 
Type C
Nonwoven material of 100% PP; 6.7 dtex, 475 g/m² fiber insert, 200 g/m² binder, flat needle non-
woven fabric; binding using needle-punching and back impregnation; the back can, if required in
the drawing, include an anti-slip coating acc. to the release; the nonwoven material can be bonded
with a mixed-fiber hardened felt cardboard acc. to TL 524 13.
3.9.5 
Type D
Nonwoven material of 100% PP; 11 dtex, 450 g/m² fiber insert, 30 g/m² acrylate binder; dilour; the
nonwoven material must be used with a substrate that is stable in shape, such as TL 524 22 or
TL 524 48, for requirements, see drawing and/or release.
3.9.6 
Type E
Nonwoven material of 100% PP; 6.7 dtex, 300 g/m² fiber insert, 80 g/m² binder; dilour; the non-
woven material must be used with a substrate that is stable in shape, such as TL 524 22 or
TL 524 48; for requirements, see drawing and/or release.
3.9.7 
Type F
Nonwoven material of 100% PP; 6.7 dtex, 400 g/m² fiber insert, no binder; dilour; the nonwoven
material must be used with a substrate that is stable in shape, such as TL 524 22 or TL 524 48; for
requirements, see drawing and/or release.
3.9.8 
Type G
Nonwoven material of 100% PET; 6.7 dtex, 540 g/m² fiber insert, 60 g/m² laminating fleece; dilour;
the nonwoven material must be used with a substrate that is stable in shape, such as TL 524 22 or
TL 524 48; for requirements, see drawing and/or release.

Type K

Type L

substrate that is stable in shape; for requirements, see drawing and/or release.
3.9.13 
Type M
Nonwoven material of 100% PP (as for type J); dilour; sheared; 6.7 dtex; 400 g/m² fiber insert;
200 g/m² SBR binder; 120 g/m² PE and 80 g/m² nonwoven cover. When this material is used with a
substrate with a stable shape, the requirements in the drawing and/or release apply.

3.18 
Dimensional change after aging in water
Acc. to section 4.6: Must show no noticeable distortions and no rolling up after aging.
3.18.1 
Measured wet
Length and width ≤ 0.5% with respect to initial condition.
3.18.2 
After aging at elevated temperature and subsequent acclimatization
Length ≤ 1.0% and width ≤ 0.5% with respect to initial condition.
3.19 
Testing for waviness and bulging due to water penetration
Acc. to section 4.7: after wetting and drying at room temperature, no waviness or bulging may oc-
cur.
3.20 
Behavior after aging at low temperature/ball-drop test
Acc. to PV 3905 and section 4.8: no tearing or breaking of the latex backing.
3.21 
Behavior upon treatment with detergent
The material must be washable with an aqueous surfactant solution and conventional organic sol-
vents, e.g., petroleum ether (special boiling point spirit II acc. to DIN 51 631, 80/100° C) without
forming a ring.
3.21.1 
Behavior with respect to petroleum ether
After petroleum ether has been allowed to evaporate from an already wetted nonwoven surface of
approx. 50 cm², there must be no ring formation. No color change may appear on the unused ma-
terial during cleaning tests with a white cloth saturated with petroleum ether, nor may bleeding of
the cloth occur.
3.22 
Burning behavior acc. to US Regulation 571.302
Acc. to TL 1010 (if specified in drawing and/or release).

Notes on testing
4.1 
Maximum tensile strength
Acc. to DIN 53 857–1, page 2; traverse speed 100 mm/min; free clamping length (measurement
length) 100 mm.
4.2 
Tear propagation strength
Acc. to DIN 53 859–4, traverse speed 100 mm/min. Not only the peak values but also the entire
scatter range of the tear propagation strength are considered in the evaluation.
4.3 
Crockmeter test
A minimum of 1 specimen is exposed per orientation. Bleeding of the rubbing cloth is not evalu-
ated.

4.4 
Filter paper test
A piece of filter paper, previously immersed in deionized water (Schleicher & Schüll Blauband 589),
shall be placed on a section of nonwoven fabric of (50 x 50) mm; these together shall then be
loaded between two glass plates with 50 N for 60 min. The filter paper is evaluated for bleeding
based on the EN 20 105-A03 gray-scale level.
4.5 
Rotary abrasion test
Colorfastness to rubbing on 100 cm² round specimens of the nonwoven fabric after 1,000 revolu-
tions with the Schopper machine against undyed nettle cloth.
Specimen curvature
5 mm
Abrasion surface
50 cm²
Load
9.81 N
Specimen size
approx. 20 x 9.5 cm
The nettle cloth specimen (ECE cotton fabric acc. to EN ISO 105–X12) is clamped into the holding
fixture on the test machine together with a wool felt pad of the same size acc. to TL 380. The nettle
cloth is evaluated for bleeding using the EN 20 105–A03 gray scale.
4.6 
Dimensional change after aging in water
A nonwoven fabric section of at least 300 x 300 mm is aged in water for 1 h at room temperature,
and, after allowing the excess water to drip off, dried for 24 h at +90° C in mechanically circulated
air and subsequently acclimated for 24 h. If the part under test is a trim-wheel housing, type B, the
specimens must be taken from outside the locker area.
4.7 
Testing for waviness
A nonwoven fabric section of at least 500 x 500 mm is laid out flat. In the center of the specimen,
approx. 50 cm³ of water is applied to an area of approx. 100 x 100 mm.
4.8 
Ball drop test
Drop height 230 mm, without hardboard plate backing, striking the surface.
