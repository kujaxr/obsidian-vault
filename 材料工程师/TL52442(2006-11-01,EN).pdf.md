Scope 
This TL standard specifies the material requirements for shape-retaining floor coverings made of 
two-layer nonwoven fabric. 
2 
Description 
Description example for floor covering with weight-reduced nonwoven fabric substrate: 
Floor covering according to TL 52442-B 
3 
Requirements 
3.1 
General requirements 
Approval of first supply and changes according to VW 01155. 
Emission behavior according to VW 50180. Odor testing shall be performed according to variants 
C2 and C3. 
Resistance to open-air weathering acc. to VW 50185. 
Colorimetric evaluation according to VW 50190. 
Avoidance of hazardous substances according to VW 91101. 
Required minimum part quantity for complete testing: 5 molded parts. 
The specified values apply to each individual measurement. 
The English translation is believed to be accurate. In case of discrepancies the German version shall govern.
Numerical notation acc. to ISO practice (see VW 01000).

3.2 
Appearance 
The composite molded part shall maintain its dimensional and functional integrity during 
transportation, storage, installation and subsequent service in the temperature range from -30 °C to 
+90 °C as well as under climatic conditions of +55 °C and 95% relative humidity. 
Delamination is not permitted. 
The material shall be processible by cutting, i.e. it must not fray at the edges during processing 
(stamping, cutting) or in later use. 
The material shall be resistant to mold fungus in a moist heat climate. 
The system structure shall be high-frequency weldable, i.e., it must not contain substances or 
foreign matter that will adversely affect the HF weldability or the durability of the weld. 
The surface of the molded parts shall exhibit a fleecy character according to the original sample. 
3.3 
Installation characteristics 
The molded carpet part must fit the contours of the vehicle interior (floor) without tearing or 
wrinkling; hollow areas are not permitted. 
3.4 
Physiological compatibility 
The material must be physiologically safe; i.e., it must not induce skin irritation or produce 
objectionable odors. 
Substances that are emitted at elevated temperatures shall be toxicologically safe. 
3.5 
Conditioning 
Prior to testing, the specimens must be conditioned for at least 24 h in the DIN 50014 - 23/50-2 
standard climate.

5 
Notes on testing 
5.1 
Weight per unit area 
Testing acc. to DIN EN 29073-1 with at least 5 round specimens measuring 100 cm2. Specimens 
distributed over the width of the roll or molded part. 
5.2 
Tear strength 
Testing acc. to DIN EN ISO 13934-1; traverse speed 100 mm/min, free clamping length 100 mm. 
5.3 
Tear propagation strength 
Testing acc. to DIN EN ISO 13937-2, traverse speed 100 mm/min. Evaluation acc. to PV 2034. 
5.4 
Rotary abrasion test 
Testing acc. to PV 3908 with a pressure force of 9,81 N. 
5.5 
Abrasion resistance 
Determination of the abrasion resistance (Taber abrader test) with grinding wheels type H18 and 
an attached weight of 1 000 g, number of cycles 1 000. 
5.6 
Colorfastness to abrasion 
Colorfastness to rubbing on round specimens measuring 100 cm2 with the Schopper machine 
against undyed nettle cloth (ECE cotton nettle cloth according to DIN EN ISO 105-X12) after 1 000 
revolutions.

5.7 
Behavior after aging 
Testing for shape stability is preferably performed in simulated as-installed position with a 
specimen support device (support core). 
Measuring points: flat floor area acc. to specification. 
5.8 
Aging in water and subsequent aging at elevated temperature 
Test acc. to SAE J883, but at +90 °C. 
A nonwoven fabric section of at least 300 mm x 300 mm is aged in water for 1 hour at room 
temperature; after the water is allowed to drip off, it is dried for 16 h at 90 °C and then conditioned 
for 24 h in the DIN 50014  23/50-2 standard climate.

5.9 
Ball-drop test 
Impact on surface from a drop height of 230 mm, without wood fiberboard support. 
5.10 
Bending behavior 
Manual bending around a bending angle of 180°; specimen width approx. 50 mm. 
5.11 
Behavior upon treatment with water 
Approx. 50 cm3 water is applied to the center of an approx. 100 x 100 mm area of a flat carpet 
section that measures at least 300 x 300 mm. 
The specimen is subsequently dried at room temperature (23 ± 2) °C. 
5.12 
Behavior upon treatment with petroleum ether 
Special boiling point spirit II acc. to DIN 51631; +80/+100 °C. 
5.13 
Behavior with view to mold fungus growth 
A specimen of 50 x 100 mm is aged at room temperature in a vessel with 1 l capacity (test vessel 
acc. to PV 3900). 
A second test vessel is filled with approx. 50 ml of water. 
A specimen with the same dimensions is placed in the test vessel so that the lower edge of the 
specimen is approx. 20 mm above the water surface. 
This can be achieved by suspending the specimen in the vessel or attaching it to a spacer.
